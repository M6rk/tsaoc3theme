Frequently Asked Questions

There are many ways to get involved! We offer volunteer opportunities in our Community Services programs, including food assistance, clothing distribution, and support for families in need. You can also participate in our special events and fundraising activities throughout the year.

No, you do not need to be a Christian to volunteer at The Salvation Army. We welcome individuals from all backgrounds and beliefs to join us in serving our community. Our primary requirement is a compassionate heart and a willingness to help those in need.

The funds raised through our events and donations are allocated to various programs and services in the Central Okanagan. This includes community outreach, emergency assistance, gifts of hope toy program, and other vital services that help those in need.

There is no minimum number of hours required to volunteer. We appreciate any time you can give, whether it's a few hours a month or a more regular commitment. Every bit of help makes a difference!

This commitment not only helps us serve the community better but also provides you with valuable experience and skills that can enhance your resume. Many of our volunteers have found that their time with us has opened doors to new job opportunities and personal growth.

Our main Worship Service is held every Sunday at 10:30 AM. We welcome people of all ages and backgrounds to join us for a time of worship, fellowship, and spiritual growth. The service typically lasts about 90 minutes and includes music, prayer, and a message from God's Word.

Yes! We have Kelsa Kids, a wonderful children's program that runs during our Sunday morning worship service. Children participate in age-appropriate activities, games, songs, and Bible stories that help them learn about God's love in fun and creative ways. All children are welcome, and our trained volunteers ensure a safe and nurturing environment.

We support individuals and families who are experiencing crisis through our Casework Empowerment model. This program involves working one-on-one with a caseworker to develop an individualized plan that helps people move toward stability and long-term goals. As part of that support, we offer clothing vouchers and other practical resources to participants based on their specific needs. These vouchers allow individuals to select items from our Thrift Store at no cost to them, ensuring they receive what’s most appropriate for their situation. While we aren’t able to match donations directly to individuals due to privacy and logistical reasons, your donation does directly support people in crisis through this voucher system. Every item you give helps us provide dignity and choice to those we serve.
